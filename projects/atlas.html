ATLAS Passive Knee Exoskeleton

A senior design entrepreneurship project focused on passive knee assistance, mechanical design, prototyping, and product development.

All project areas Contact

Project Overview

ATLAS was a senior design entrepreneurship project focused on developing a passive knee exoskeleton for hikers and active users.

  • The goal was to reduce knee strain using a passive mechanical assist system.
  • The project combined mechanical design, prototyping, testing planning, and product thinking.
  • The design had to balance weight, cost, adjustability, comfort, and practical use.

Engineering Problem

Hiking, especially downhill or over long distances, can create repeated knee loading and discomfort.

  • The design explored whether a passive device could support knee extension without motors or batteries.
  • The challenge was making the assist useful without making the system bulky or complicated.
  • This required thinking through human motion, spring behavior, and mechanical interfaces.

My Role

I worked as a design lead and helped move the project from concept toward prototype.

  • Led mechanical concept development and prototype direction.
  • Worked through mechanism layout, CAD decisions, and design tradeoffs.
  • Helped define requirements around weight, adjustability, cost, and manufacturability.

What This Shows

This project shows my ability to work through an open-ended mechanical design problem from the user need toward an actual product direction.

  • Mechanical design from a real user problem.
  • Prototype-focused thinking instead of only theoretical design.
  • Ability to balance performance, cost, manufacturability, and usability.
