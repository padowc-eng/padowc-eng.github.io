Project overview

ATLAS was a senior design entrepreneurship project focused on developing a passive knee exoskeleton for hikers and active users. The design goal was to create a mechanical assist system that could reduce knee strain without relying on motors, batteries, or a complicated control system.

Problem being solved

Hiking, especially downhill or over long distances, can create repeated knee loading. The project explored whether a passive mechanical system could support knee extension and reduce strain while still feeling practical to wear.

My role

  • Led mechanical concept development and prototype direction.
  • Worked through mechanism layout, assist behavior, CAD decisions, and design tradeoffs.
  • Helped define requirements around weight, adjustability, cost, manufacturability, and usability.
  • Presented design decisions through reviews and entrepreneurship-focused development.

Engineering work

Mechanism thinking

The concept focused on passive assistance during knee motion. The design needed to store and return energy in a useful way without fighting the user or making the system too complicated to wear.

Prototype constraints

The design had to be judged against real constraints: weight, fit, comfort, cost, durability, manufacturability, and whether someone would actually use it outdoors.

Product direction

Because this was entrepreneurship-focused, the work also included user need, market fit, cost targets, and what a realistic first prototype could prove.
